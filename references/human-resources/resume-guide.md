# HR Resume Writing Guide

> From the perspective of an experienced HR hiring manager — the underlying logic

## Core Principles

### How HR Evaluates HR Resumes

When HR screens peers, the internal judgment criteria are:

| Resume claim | HR's actual question |
|---|---|
| "Can recruit" | How many hires? Time-to-fill? Quality? |
| "Understands employee relations" | What tricky cases? Arbitration? Termination? Injury? |
| "Handled compensation" | How many employees? What system? Error rate? |
| "Has BP mindset" | Do you understand business? Which department? What did you drive? |

**Conclusion:** Don't write "responsible for recruiting" or "responsible for training." Write **what problem you solved, what result you delivered, and what methodology you institutionalized.**

### Universal Formula

```
[Action Object] + [Quantified Result] + [Core Method/Tool] + [Verifiable Improvement]
```

## Per-Module Breakdown

### Recruitment

**Weak:**
> Responsible for all-position recruitment, including posting jobs, screening resumes, scheduling interviews, following up on offers, meeting department needs.

**Strong:**

```
Recruitment Delivery | 86 hires/year (sales/ops/engineering), avg. cycle 18 days, probation pass rate 92%
Hard-to-fill roles: Led 3 Regional BD Manager roles via Mapping + competitor talent pool, 4 hires in 35 days
Channel optimization: Dropped low-ROI platform (4500 CNY/hire), shifted to niche communities + referral incentives, referral rate 15% → 41%, saved 72K/year
Process improvement: Built structured interview question bank, interviewer satisfaction 67% → 89%, new hire 3-month success rate +23%
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Lead with results: headcount + cycle time + quality (probation pass rate)
- Breakthrough cases: how you solved hard-to-fill roles (Mapping / competitors / new channels)
- Data closed-loop: cost savings, efficiency gains, retention improvement

### Employee Relations / Labor Relations

**Weak:**
> Responsible for onboarding/offboarding procedures, file management, employee inquiries, assisted with labor disputes.

**Strong:**

```
Labor Relations Risk Control | Optimized full-cycle process, exit dispute rate 12% → 2.3%
Critical case: Led probation-incompetence termination case independently, built evidence chain, negotiated exit, 0 compensation
Process overhaul: Found 40% missing JD signatures, pushed e-sign + SOP, compliance → 100%
Exit management: Designed "risk signal" tiered matrix for exit interviews, flagged 8 potential disputes, 5 resolved amicably
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Specific case types: "probation incompetence termination," "misconduct dismissal," "work injury negotiation"
- Your role: led independently or assisted? What legal clauses and evidence?
- Institutional output: SOPs, checklists, matrices — turning case experience into company rules

### HRBP / Generalist

**Weak:**
> Supported engineering HR work including recruitment, performance, employee relations, team building, assisted business leaders with team management.

**Strong:**

```
HRBP | Supported 120-person R&D center (Java / Product / QA)
Org diagnosis: Exit analysis showed 35% backend dev turnover at 6-12 months, root cause was broken mentorship.
  Drove 1-on-1 mentoring + KPI tie-in, turnover → 12% in 6 months
Talent review: Ran tech-sequence calibration meeting, identified 8 high-potentials (3 promoted), 2 on PIP (1 retained)
Business alignment: Addressed delivery delays with team-bonus decoupling model, on-time delivery 58% → 79%
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Business language: turnover rate, delivery rate, promotion success — not "organized team building"
- Diagnose → Act → Result: find problem → solution → quantified improvement
- Influence on business leaders: what decisions did you drive business leaders to make

### Training & Development

**Strong example:**

```
New Hire Training | 120 participants, 3-month retention 68% → 89%
Curriculum redesign: 3-day lecture → "2-day courses + 1-day business simulation", NPS 6.2 → 8.5
Mentor credit system: 5 credits per mentee redeemable for annual recognition, participation 40% → 95%
```

## Three Don'ts vs Three Must-Dos

| Don't (HR sees through it) | Must (shows professionalism) |
|---|---|
| "Responsible for full recruitment process" | "86 hires/year, avg. 18-day time-to-fill" |
| "Proficient in office software" | "Built turnover prediction model in Excel (82% accuracy)" |
| "Assisted with employee relations" | "Led XX labor dispute independently, saved XX / avoided compensation" |

## Company Type Focus

| Company Type | What they value | Resume emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Large companies / enterprises | Data-driven decisions, compliance awareness | Labor dispute cases, data-driven decision making |
| Small/Mid companies | Hit-the-ground-running, fill hard roles | Recruitment breakthroughs, multi-role systematic expression |

## ATS Strategy

Your resume may pass through automated screening or quick review by non-HR managers:

1. Pick 5-10 keywords from your target module that genuinely match your skills
2. Weave keywords **naturally** into experience bullets — never dump them at the end
3. File name: `Name-Role.pdf` (never `.doc`, formatting breaks)
