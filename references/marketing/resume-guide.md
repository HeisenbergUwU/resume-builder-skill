# Marketing & Operations Resume Writing Guide

> From the perspective of an experienced Marketing Hiring Manager

## Core Principles

### How Hiring Managers Evaluate Marketing Resumes

When hiring managers screen marketing/operations candidates, the internal criteria are:

| Resume claim | HM's actual question |
|---|---|
| "Brand marketing" | Impressions? Awareness lift? Budget size? |
| "WeChat/social media ops" | Followers? Open rate? Conversion? Growth source? |
| "Growth marketing" | CAC? Retention? LTV? How much growth? |
| "Event planning" | Scale? ROI? New users? Revenue? |

**Conclusion:** Don't write "responsible for operations" or "responsible for promotion." Write **what growth you drove, what budget you managed, what conversions you delivered, and what methodology you institutionalized.**

### Universal Formula

```
[Business Metric] + [Action] + [Quantified Result] + [Methodology]
```

## Per-Module Breakdown

### Digital Marketing / Growth

**Weak:**
> Responsible for social media operations across WeChat, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, including content publishing and follower growth.

**Strong:**

```
Growth Marketing | 120K acquired users/year, CAC from ¥180 → ¥65, ROI 1:4.2
Channel matrix: Built "Xiaohongshu seeding + Douyin video + paid ads" funnel, 30K leads/month
Content growth: 23 viral posts (peak 5.8M impressions), organic traffic share 28% → 61%
Paid optimization: 14 landing page A/B tests, conversion rate 3.2% → 7.8%, saved ¥45K/month
Viral loop: Designed "invite-for-membership" referral, K-factor 1.32, 42K new users in 30 days
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Lead with metrics: users acquired, CAC, ROI, conversion rate, LTV
- Channel expertise: specific channels + contribution per channel + optimization actions
- Growth methodology: referral mechanics, A/B testing, attribution models

### Brand Marketing

**Weak:**
> Responsible for brand promotion, brand strategy, brand image maintenance, and brand awareness.

**Strong:**

```
Brand Marketing | Brand search volume +340%, awareness score 72→89
Brand campaign: Led annual "XX Theme" campaign, 230M impressions, 3 trending Weibo topics
KOL matrix: Built 200+ KOL network (tiered: top/mid/KOC), CPM per collaboration -40%
Co-branding: Led X-brand collab, 560K combined new followers, collab product sold out in 12 min
Media relations: Maintained 80+ core media contacts, 120+ positive reports/year, 100% 24h crisis response
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Brand metrics: search volume, awareness, positive sentiment rate
- Campaign scale: impressions, engagement, trending topics, media coverage
- Asset accumulation: KOL database, media relationships, partner brands

### User Operations / Community Ops

**Weak:**
> Responsible for user community operations, user relationship maintenance, user engagement and retention.

**Strong:**

```
User Operations | 50K+ users across communities, 30-day retention 41%, ARPU ¥128
Segmentation: Built RFM model, top 10% users drive 62% revenue
Community SOP: Designed "7-day onboarding" SOP, Week-1 active rate 23% → 58%
User events: Monthly themed check-in events, 34% participation, 1,200+ UGC posts/month
Churn prediction: Built churn risk model (79% accuracy), recovered 860 high-value users, ¥320K revenue saved
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Ops metrics: retention, ARPU, engagement, churn rate
- Segmentation methodology: RFM, user personas, lifecycle management
- Tools/SOPs: automated touchpoints, prediction models, SOP workflows

### Content Marketing

**Weak:**
> Responsible for product content marketing, including article writing, video production, and social media.

**Strong:**

```
Content Marketing | 360 pieces/year, 80M total impressions, content-driven acquisition 35%
SEO content: 120 long-tail keyword articles, organic traffic +420%, 150K monthly organic visits
Video content: Douyin + Video Account, 280K combined followers, peak 3.2M views/video
Content ops system: Topic library (200+ tags) + distribution SOP, per-person output +180%
Whitepapers: 3 industry reports, 42K downloads, 3,800 MQLs converted
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Content metrics: impressions, views, organic traffic growth, acquisition share
- Distribution: per-platform data, follower growth, viral rate
- Systemization: content hub, topic library, distribution SOPs

### Product Marketing

**Weak:**
> Responsible for product launch promotion, value proposition writing, and sales team training.

**Strong:**

```
Product Marketing | Led GTM for 4 core products, Month-1 GMV ¥28M
Go-to-Market: XX product GTM strategy, 50K Week-1 signups, 8.3% pay conversion
Sales enablement: Battle Cards + Demo Video, deal cycle shortened by 22%
Competitive analysis: Quarterly report covering 8 competitors, pricing adjustment → margin +5.2%
Customer cases: 12 flagship case studies, sales win rate 31% → 44%
```

**What to emphasize:**
- Launch outcomes: Month-1/Quarter-1 revenue, users, conversion rate
- Sales enablement: tools produced, deal cycle, win rate
- Market insight: competitive analysis, pricing strategy, customer cases

## Three Don'ts vs Three Must-Dos

| Don't (HM sees through it) | Must (shows professionalism) |
|---|---|
| "Responsible for social media ops" | "280K followers, 8M monthly impressions, 35% content-driven acquisition" |
| "Planned online events" | "Referral campaign, K-factor 1.32, 42K new users in 30 days" |
| "Improved brand awareness" | "Brand search volume +340%, awareness score 72→89" |

## ATS Strategy

Your resume may pass through automated screening or quick review by non-technical managers:

1. Pick 5-10 keywords from your target module that genuinely match your skills
2. Weave keywords **naturally** into experience bullets — never dump them at the end
3. File name: `Name-Role.pdf` (never `.doc`, formatting breaks)
